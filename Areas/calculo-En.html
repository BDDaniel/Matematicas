Calculation


In general, the term calculation refers to the result corresponding to the action of calculating. Calculate, on the other hand, consists of performing the necessary operations to foresee the result of a previously conceived action, or to know the consequences that can be derived from previously known data.
However, the most common use of the term "calculus" is the logical-mathematical one. From this perspective, calculus consists of a mechanical procedure or algorithm, by means of which we can know the consequences derived from previously known variables duly formalized and symbolized.

Limits and continuity

A limit is the touch key that formalizes the intuitive notion of approaching a particular point of a sequence or function, as the parameters of that sequence or function approach a certain value.

The derivative of a function is the instantaneous rate of change with which the value of that mathematical function varies as the value of its independent variable changes.

Integration

Integration is a fundamental concept of calculus and mathematical analysis. Basically, an integral is a generalization of the sum of infinitely many, infinitesimally small summands: a continuous sum.
