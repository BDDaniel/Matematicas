Algebra


Algebra is the branch of mathematics that studies the combination of elements of abstract structures according to certain rules. Originally these elements could be interpreted as numbers or quantities, so that algebra in a way was originally a generalization and extension of arithmetic. In modern algebra there are areas of algebra that can in no way be considered extensions of arithmetic (abstract algebra, homological algebra, exterior algebra, etc.).
