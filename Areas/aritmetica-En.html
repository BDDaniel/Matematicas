Arithmetic


Arithmetic is the branch of mathematics whose object of study is numbers and the elementary operations performed with them: addition, subtraction, multiplication and division. As in other areas of Mathematics, such as Algebra or Geometry, the meaning of "Arithmetic" has been evolving with the broad and diversified development of the sciences. Originally, Arithmetic was developed in a formal way in Ancient Greece, with the refinement of mathematical rigor and proofs, and its extension to the different disciplines of the "Natural Sciences". Nowadays, it can refer to Elementary Arithmetic, focused on the teaching of Basic Mathematics; also to the set that gathers Arithmetic Calculus and Mathematical Operations, specifically, the four Basic Operations applied either to numbers (natural numbers, whole numbers, fractional numbers and decimal numbers, etc.) or to more abstract mathematical entities (matrices, operators, etc.); also to the so-called high arithmetic, known as Number Theory.

Addition and subtraction

Addition is also called addition. There are two parts to addition: the addends and the sum or total, which is the result. You can associate the addends in different ways to add and the sum or total will be the same.

Multiplication and division

Multiplication and division are closely related since division is the inverse of multiplication. In division we seek to separate into equal groups, while in multiplication we seek to unite or join into equal groups.

A negative number is any number whose value is less than zero and, therefore, less than the other positive numbers.

Fractions

Every fraction is a division and every division is a fraction. Because of that a division can be converted into a fraction to be simplified. Fractions can be represented as (a÷b) or (a/b) in a mathematical operation.

Decimals

Decimal numbers are values that represent numbers smaller than unity. Each decimal number has a whole number part and a decimal part, where the whole number part is to the left of the decimal point and the decimal part is to the right.
